About

Bringing Behavioral Health Into Primary Care

I'm a clinical psychologist and the Director of Integrated Behavioral Health in the Department of Family Medicine at the University of Virginia School of Medicine. In that role, I oversee clinical services, trainee education, and strategic development for UVA's Primary Care Behavioral Health (PCBH) program across multiple clinic sites.

My work sits at the intersection of clinical care, health systems, and implementation science. I help primary care practices build behavioral health programs that are sustainable, evidence-based, and actually work in the reality of busy clinical environments — not just in ideal conditions.

Outside of UVA, I consult with health systems and primary care organizations on behavioral health integration strategy, model selection, and program implementation. I bring a practitioner's perspective to implementation work: I've built these programs from the inside, supervised the clinicians who staff them, and navigated the operational and cultural challenges that don't show up in the literature.

Consulting

Behavioral Health Integration Advisory

I work with health systems, primary care organizations, and practice groups to design and build integrated behavioral health programs. Engagements are typically project-based or retainer, scoped to the organization's stage and needs.
