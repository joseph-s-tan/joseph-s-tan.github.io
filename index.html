Bringing Behavioral Health Into Primary Care

I'm a clinical psychologist working at the intersection of clinical care, health systems, and implementation science.

I serve as Director of Integrated Behavioral Health in the Department of Family Medicine at the University of Virginia School of Medicine, where I oversee clinical services, trainee education, and strategic development for UVA's Primary Care Behavioral Health (PCBH) program across multiple clinic sites.

My interest is in how primary care practices build behavioral health programs that are sustainable, evidence-based, and actually work in the reality of busy clinical environments — not just in ideal conditions. I also work externally with health systems and primary care organizations on behavioral health integration strategy, model selection, and program development.

I write about these topics because most of what gets published about integrated behavioral health is either too abstract to be actionable or too narrow to generalize. There's a gap between the evidence base and what practitioners actually need to build good programs.
